Questions and Answers

Why should my son join a fraternity?


There are many benefits to joining a fraternity received both during undergraduate years as well as post graduation. Below are just a few of said benefits.
Academics - Your son will be making the transition to college from an obviously different atmosphere. While adjustment may be harder on some than others, having the resource of a fraternity full of brothers who have not only taken the same classes, but have experience in the college environment to depend on, can greatly reduce the difficulty of that transition. We have brothers in all areas of study from business to multiple engineering fields and teaching to pre-pharmacy.
Lifelong Friends - Your son will be making plenty of new friends at college, but few will be lifelong friends as those gained a fraternity. From vacationing with brothers on spring break to having a network on which to rely for jobs, interviews, and contacts, our fraternity is a lifelong resource that can be found all over the country and even some other countries.
Leadership - Our fraternity alone has about 30 leadership positions. Those are 30 leadership positions that can not only help your son develop character, but those are 30 leadership positions which look great on résumés.

What is the time commitment my son can expect from your fraternity?


When it comes to a fraternity, the amount of time you put in will rely solely on how much time the member wishes to put in. A general member can be expected to put in about 3 to 4 hours a week, while a chairperson and executive member can expect to put in an hour to 3 hours more per week. This time is spent in chapter meetings, committee meetings, and general workshops. However, if the member wishes, additional time will be spent at brotherhood events, intramural sports, as well as social events.

What is the cost my son/I can expect to pay?


Sigma Pi retains some of the lowest costs of any fraternity nationally. The Greek Experience is an investment in your student's future. The leadership skills, the academic assistance, and friendships will benefit your child beyond their college days. The perception that fraternities are only an option for “rich” students is widespread and false. Greek organizations are quite affordable and fees go to services that will positively impact your son. Many students work to supplement funding for their dues. Member’s dues directly support the betterment of the chapter and the national organization. To assist members, chapters may offer various scholarships and grants. We encourage your son to ask questions related to finances during the recruitment process. We also encourage you to be ”hands-on” in this decision if you have any questions about the obligations regarding finances. Initially, there is a pledge fee of $150, there is then an initiation fee of $350. Once initiated, brothers pay dues once semesterly which vary depending on whether or not they live in our chapter house. It can be expected that dues will range from $360 to $500 per semester.

Is there an alcohol policy?


Each chapter has an alcohol policy in which there are yearly educational programs that the chapter is required to present to its members. The international philanthropy of Sigma Pi is the Sam Spady Foundation (www.samspadyfoundation.org) whose mission is to educate all parents and students on the dangers of alcohol, specifically high-risk consumption, and the signs and symptoms of alcohol poisoning.

 
What about hazing?


Sigma Pi strictly prohibits all forms of hazing. A holistic definition of hazing can also be found in every University’s Code of Polices and Regulations Applying to All Students. The Fraternity investigates all allegations.


What is my role as a parent?


Take the time to find out more about the chapter your son is joining. Ask questions about what the organizations will offer your child and allow them to make the best decision for themselves. Once your child chooses to join a chapter, there will be opportunities for Mom’s and Dad’s weekend activities, as well as football games, or a number of other activities.
As you look into the Greek Community with your son consider the following information compiled by national studies: 
Fraternity affiliation can positively influence retention through graduation. Fraternity affiliation directly impacts campus involvement and overall student satisfaction with college. Fraternity affiliation can positively influence involvement in civic organizations after college. Some statistics compiled by the North-American Interfraternity Conference include:

  • 48% of all US Presidents have been Greek
  • 42% of US Senators are Greek
  • 30% of US Congressmen are Greek
  • 40% of all US Supreme Court Justices have been Greek
  • 30% of Fortune 500 Executives are Greek

And Greeks only make up 3% of the US population!
